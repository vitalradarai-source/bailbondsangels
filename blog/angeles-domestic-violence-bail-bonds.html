What Happens After a DV Arrest in Los Angeles

Under California Penal Code § 836(c), law enforcement officers responding to a domestic disturbance are required to make an arrest when they have probable cause to believe a DV offense occurred. This means even if the reporting party does not want to press charges, the officer may — and typically will — make an arrest anyway.

After the arrest, LAPD officers or LA County Sheriff's deputies transport the defendant to the nearest booking facility. For most of LA County, that means Twin Towers Correctional Facility (450 Bauchet St, Los Angeles, CA 90012) or Men's Central Jail (441 Bauchet St) directly adjacent to it. Both facilities are operated by the Los Angeles County Sheriff's Department and share the same downtown booking complex.

Law enforcement will also issue an Emergency Protective Order (EPO) at the scene. An EPO is a short-term order — typically 5 to 7 days — prohibiting the arrested person from contacting the protected party. The EPO does not affect bail eligibility. Bail can still be posted as soon as booking is complete.

Twin Towers is the primary intake and booking facility for LA County. It processes tens of thousands of arrests per year, which means booking times vary significantly by time of day and day of week. During off-peak hours, booking can be completed in 4 to 6 hours. During busy weekend nights or after large-scale incidents, processing can stretch to 8 to 10 hours or longer.

Once a person clears booking — fingerprints, photographs, criminal history check, and charge entry into the system — their bail amount is set according to the LA County bail schedule. At that point, a bail bond can be posted immediately. There is no mandatory waiting period beyond the booking process itself.

Important: There is no automatic 72-hour hold for domestic violence arrests in California. Bail can be posted as soon as the booking process is complete — often within hours of arrest.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does booking take at Twin Towers after a DV arrest?

Booking at Twin Towers typically takes 4 to 10 hours depending on the time of day and how busy the facility is. Weekend nights tend to be the slowest. The booking process includes fingerprinting, photographing, a criminal history check, and official entry of charges into the system. Bail can be posted as soon as that process is complete.

Can bail be posted immediately after a domestic violence arrest?

Yes. Once the booking process is complete and a bail amount has been set from the LA County bail schedule, a bail bond can be posted right away. There is no mandatory 72-hour hold or automatic waiting period for DV arrests in California. Angels Bail Bonds can begin the paperwork process as soon as the defendant appears in the system.

What does the 10% bail bond premium mean?

California law caps bail bond premiums at 10% of the total bail amount. If bail is set at $50,000, you pay $5,000 to the bail bondsman. That fee is non-refundable regardless of the case outcome — it is the cost of the bonding service. In exchange, the bondsman posts a surety bond for the full $50,000 with the jail, securing the defendant's release without requiring you to post the full amount in cash.

Does an Emergency Protective Order prevent bail from being posted?

No. An Emergency Protective Order (EPO) is a civil order that restricts contact between the defendant and the protected party. It does not affect the defendant's right to bail. Bail can be posted even while an EPO is active. However, once released, the defendant must comply with all EPO conditions — violating an EPO is a separate criminal offense that can result in additional charges and a bail hold.
