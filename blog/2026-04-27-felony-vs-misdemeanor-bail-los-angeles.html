Felony vs Misdemeanor Bail in Los Angeles — Why the Charge Type Changes Everything

TL;DR

  • Misdemeanor (CA PC §17): up to 1 year county jail, fine up to $2,000
  • Felony: 16 months or more state prison; can be life or death for capital crimes
  • Misdemeanor LA bail: typically $500 - $25,000 (set by schedule)
  • Felony LA bail: typically $25,000 - $1M+ (often requires judge's hearing)
  • Felonies usually transfer to Twin Towers or Men's/Women's Central Jail
  • Wobblers (PC 245, 273.5, 459, 470) — prosecutor decides which way to file

If a loved one was just arrested in Los Angeles County, one of the first things you'll hear is whether the charge is a misdemeanor or a felony. That single classification changes everything about what comes next — bail amount, custody location, court speed, attorney needs, and long-term consequences. This guide explains how the two differ in California law and how it affects your bail bond decisions.

The Legal Definition

Under California Penal Code §17:

  • Misdemeanor: Punishable by up to 1 year in county jail and/or a fine up to $1,000-$2,000 depending on the offense
  • Felony: Punishable by 16 months, 2 years, or 3 years in state prison (often longer); capital crimes (murder, etc.) carry life imprisonment or death
  • Infraction: Punishable only by fine — typically minor traffic or code violations, no jail time, no bail

Approximate bail amounts based on LA Felony Bail Schedule. Judge can adjust higher or lower based on circumstances.

  • VC §23152 DUI (misdemeanor first offense) — $5,000 to $25,000
  • PC §187 murder (felony) — $1,000,000 or no bail

Why Wobblers Are Tricky

A "wobbler" is a charge the prosecutor can file as either a misdemeanor or a felony. Common wobblers in LA County include PC §245, §273.5, §459, §470, §647(f) (drunk in public), and §484 (theft). The prosecutor's decision depends on:

  • Defendant's prior record
  • Severity of injury or property damage
  • Whether a weapon was involved
  • Vulnerability of the victim (minor, elderly, disabled)
  • Aggravating circumstances at the scene

For wobblers, bail is initially set at the felony amount pending the prosecutor's filing decision (usually within 48-72 hours of arrest). Once formally filed:

  • Filed as misdemeanor: Bail typically drops, conditions relax
  • Filed as felony: Higher bail stands; case proceeds to felony arraignment at Foltz

How Charge Type Affects the Bail Bond Process

1. Speed of Release

Misdemeanor bail follows the schedule and can often be posted within hours of booking. Felony bail typically requires a judge's hearing — within 48 hours of arrest excluding weekends, per CA PC §825. The defendant waits in custody for that hearing.

2. Premium and Cosigner Requirements

The 10% premium under CA Insurance Code §1800.4 applies to both. But on a $100,000 felony bail vs a $10,000 misdemeanor bail:

  • Misdemeanor: $1,000 premium — payment plan often unnecessary
  • Felony: $10,000 premium — payment plans and collateral common

3. Bondsman Risk and Approval

Felony cases carry higher forfeiture risk (defendant has more incentive to skip). Bondsmen scrutinize cosigner financial standing more carefully on felonies. 4. Court of Arraignment

  • Misdemeanor arraignment: Local courthouse (Pomona, Long Beach, Van Nuys, etc.)
  • Felony arraignment: Clara Shortridge Foltz Criminal Justice Center, 210 W Temple St, Los Angeles, CA 90012 — the main LA felony court

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between misdemeanor and felony in California?

Per CA PC §17: misdemeanor = up to 1 year county jail. Felony = 16 months or more state prison. Misdemeanors carry lower bail, faster release, less long-term impact on rights.

How much higher is felony bail in LA?

Misdemeanor: $500-$25,000. Felony: $25,000-$1M+. The 10% premium scales with the bail amount under CA Ins Code §1800.4.

Can felony bail be denied?

Per CA Constitution Art I §12, bail is generally available except for capital crimes, certain violent felonies with substantial likelihood of harm, and serious sexual assault cases.

How does processing differ?

Misdemeanor: schedule-based, 6-12 hr custody. Felony: requires judge's hearing within 48 hr, transferred to Twin Towers or Central Jail, 24-72 hr custody.

What's a wobbler?

A charge the prosecutor can file as either misdemeanor or felony based on circumstances. Common wobblers: PC 245, 273.5, 459, 470. Initially bailed at felony level, may drop after filing decision.
